Welcome!


I am currently a fourth-year PhD candidate in the Department of Chemistry at MIT, where I work with Prof. Bin Zhang and collaborate with Prof. Martin Aryee at Harvard and Prof. Bradley Berstein at Broad Institute. My research interests are physical modeling and large-scale computation of complex biological systems, e.g. human genome. It is hard to clearly define a major since massive cross-disciplinary research from different fields are involved, maybe a computational chemist uses physics and math to work on biological systems would suffice.

Before coming to MIT, I received my B.S. in Chemical Physics from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in 2016. I was awarded the 35th Guo Moruo Scholarship which is the highest honor at USTC.
